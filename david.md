# David — how to work with him

Notes for any AI collaborator on how David thinks and communicates, so responses land the first
time instead of needing translation.

## Who he is
Owner of Skyjems, a family coloured-gemstone and custom jewellery business (Toronto, est. 1967).
Deep expertise in gemstones and the jewellery trade — not a software background. He is not
"computer illiterate"; the real bottleneck is *translation latency* — the gap between having an
idea and it becoming a working thing. Collaborators should aim to shrink that gap, not route
around it.

## How he communicates
- Plain, direct language. Explain software/technical concepts in trade terms, not jargon.
- He uses inconsistent placeholders and shorthand — infer the intended meaning rather than
  stopping to ask which one he means, when it's reasonably inferable.
- Prefers being shown a small number of strong options over a long list of mediocre ones. Two
  excellent choices beats twelve pretty-good ones.
- Pictures/mockups before paragraphs, when the subject is visual.
- If he says something like "nothing is doing it for me," treat that as a design critique, not a
  request for more volume.

## How he makes decisions
- Comfort, durability, and craft beat cleverness — especially anywhere the client will physically
  touch or wear something.
- He'd rather point a client toward the right gem — even one he'd have to source, or even a
  competitor's better fit — than force a wrong one. Honesty over the sale.
- Business decisions (money, commitments to customers, anything hard to reverse) are always his
  call. AI collaborators should arrive with a recommendation, not a menu of undifferentiated
  options, and should have already done the legwork before asking him anything.
- He does not want task lists handed back to him. If a plan's output is "here's what David needs
  to do next," treat that as incomplete work, not a deliverable.

## Working style
- Comfortable being closely involved in long, iterative work sessions — happy to co-pilot,
  correct course mid-stream, and keep momentum rather than waiting for a fully-baked result.
- Values terse, direct status updates over lengthy narration.
