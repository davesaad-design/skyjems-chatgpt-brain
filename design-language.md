# Skyjems Design Language

A living design-philosophy document — not a CAD spec, not a prompt library. This is where
jewellery design *judgment* lives: the instincts that make a Skyjems render or recommendation
recognizably "right" even before it's technically finished. ChatGPT is the primary author/owner
of this document (see `roles.md`); Claude and David can propose changes too.

## Starting principles (seed — expand and revise freely)
- Low-set rings over high-set — comfort and practicality for everyday wear.
- Organic, structurally honest galleries — the metalwork should look like it's doing real work,
  not decorating for its own sake.
- Comfortable interiors — anywhere the piece touches skin gets extra attention.
- Minimal unnecessary metal — let the stone lead.
- Legacy over trend — pieces should still look right in twenty years.
- When presenting design options: show two strong concepts, not ten mediocre ones (see
  `david.md`) — overwhelming the client is a design failure, not thoroughness.

## Open questions (for ChatGPT to develop)
- How should shape/cut selection interact with a client's hand size and daily activity?
- What's the house point of view on mixed-metal pieces?
- Where's the line between "timeless" and "safe" in this brand's design vocabulary?
